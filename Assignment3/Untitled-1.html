What is Narcolepsy Type 1?

Narcolepsy Type 1 (formerly called narcolepsy with cataplexy) is a chronic neurological sleep disorder characterized by the brain's inability to regulate sleep-wake cycles normally. It is caused by the loss of hypocretin (orexin) neurons in the hypothalamus, which produce neurotransmitters that help keep us awake and alert.

Key Features:
  • Excessive Daytime Sleepiness (EDS): Overwhelming urge to sleep during the day
  • Cataplexy: Sudden loss of muscle tone triggered by strong emotions (laughter, surprise, anger)
  • Sleep Paralysis: Temporary inability to move when falling asleep or waking up
  • Hypnagogic Hallucinations: Vivid, often frightening dreams while falling asleep or waking
  • Disrupted Nighttime Sleep: Frequent awakenings during the night
