Straight talk: Narcolepsy Type 1 is a neurological condition‚Äîbasically a wiring issue in the brain. The part of your brain that's supposed to keep you alert during the day isn't producing the right chemicals (something called hypocretin). It's NOT about being lazy or staying up too late. This is a real medical condition, like diabetes or epilepsy.

The key thing is: their brain can't regulate sleep-wake cycles properly. Even if they got 10 hours of sleep, they'll still feel like they pulled an all-nighter.

  • Choppy Night Sleep: Ironically, they don't sleep well at night either. Waking up constantly.

Anyone with narcolepsy CANNOT safely operate heavy equipment, work at heights, or do any job where a sudden sleep attack or cataplexy episode could be fatal.

This includes: operating cranes, forklifts, excavators, boom lifts, working on scaffolding, roofing, steel work, or driving company vehicles.

It's not discrimination‚Äîit's physics. A 2-second cataplexy episode at the wrong moment can kill them and everyone around them.
